Visit Rwanda: Provinces and Districts

Overview of Rwanda's Administrative Divisions

Rwanda, known as the "Land of a Thousand Hills," is a vibrant East African nation with stunning landscapes ranging from volcanic mountains to savannas. Administratively, Rwanda is divided into five provinces, each further subdivided into 30 districts. Established in 2006, this structure supports decentralized governance, fostering local development and cultural preservation. Kigali, the capital, stands as its own province, serving as the economic and political hub.

Key Facts

  • Rwanda is divided into 5 provinces, including Kigali Province.
  • The country has 30 districts for administrative efficiency.
  • Provinces were reorganized in 2006 to enhance local governance.
  • Each province has a unique capital, with Kigali as the national capital.

Northern Province

The Northern Province (Kinyarwanda: Amajyaruguru) is renowned for its breathtaking volcanic landscapes, including the Virunga Mountains and Lake Burera. It is a hub for ecotourism, tea production, and agriculture. Capital: Byumba. Area: 3,276 km². Population (2022): 2,038,511. Density: 622.3 per km².

Districts

  • Burera
  • Gakenke
  • Gicumbi
  • Musanze
  • Rulindo

Eastern Province

The Eastern Province (Kinyarwanda: Iburasirazuba) spans vast savannas and wetlands, including Akagera National Park. It is vital for wildlife conservation, livestock, and crops like maize and beans. Capital: Rwamagana. Area: 9,458 km². Population (2022): 3,563,145. Density: 376.7 per km².

Districts

  • Bugesera
  • Gatsibo
  • Kayonza
  • Kirehe
  • Ngoma
  • Nyagatare
  • Rwamagana

Southern Province

The Southern Province (Kinyarwanda: Amajyepfo) features terraced hills, coffee plantations, and historical sites like the Murambi Genocide Memorial. It is a key region for coffee and pyrethrum production. Capital: Nyanza. Area: 5,963 km². Population (2022): 3,002,699. Density: 503.6 per km².

Districts

  • Gisagara
  • Huye
  • Kamonyi
  • Muhanga
  • Nyamagabe
  • Nyanza
  • Nyaruguru
  • Ruhango

Western Province

The Western Province (Kinyarwanda: Iburengerazuba) is home to Lake Kivu, rainforests, and mineral resources. It supports fishing, mining, and hydropower generation. Capital: Kibuye. Area: 5,883 km². Population (2022): 2,896,484. Density: 492.4 per km².

Districts

  • Karongi
  • Ngororero
  • Nyabihu
  • Nyamasheke
  • Rubavu
  • Rusizi
  • Rutsiro

Kigali Province

Kigali Province (Kinyarwanda: Umujyi wa Kigali) encompasses Rwanda’s capital, Kigali, the nation’s economic, political, and cultural center. It is known for rapid urbanization and modern infrastructure. Capital: Kigali City. Area: 730 km². Population (2022): 1,745,555. Density: 2,391 per km².

Districts

  • Gasabo
  • Kicukiro
  • Nyarugenge
